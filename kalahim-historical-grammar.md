There are no /ji, wu/.

* /pʲ bʲ/ > /f/, /kʲ gʲ/ > /tʲ dʲ/, /tʲ dʲ/ > /ʃ/
* /w/ > ∅ / V\_V.
* /s/ > /r/ / \_[+voi, +cons].
* /i/ > /y/ / ʷ\_.
* /ju/ > /y/.
* /k, g/ > /tʃ, dʒ/, except when \_{y, u}.
* /tʷ, dʷ/ > /t, d/ / \_{i, e}, > /k/ /\_u, > /p, b/ /\_{a, o}.
* /kʷ gʷ/ > /k, g/.
* VV > Vː.
* Geminate consonants become singleton and lengthen the preceding vowel.
* /iː, uː/ > /eː, oː/.
* /aː, eː, oː/ > /ai, ei, oi/.
* /e, o/ > /je, wo/ when stressed.
* WRITING
* /y/ > /i/.
* /ai, au/ > /e, o/.
* /oi, ou/ > /wi, o/.
* /wi/ > /we/ when unstressed.
